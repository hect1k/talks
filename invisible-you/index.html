The World You Inherit

You've grown up inside a machine - a world where every click, scroll, pause, and purchase is quietly recorded. In this Data Age, you are both the user and the product: your behavior is harvested, analyzed, and sold to shape your decisions before you even make them. This isn't just convenience; it's Surveillance Capitalism - a system where your attention, preferences, and even your emotions are monetized, often without your awareness or consent.

What is privacy?

Privacy is the power to reveal yourself selectively - to choose what parts of your life others see and what stays private. Without privacy, your autonomy disappears, leaving you vulnerable to control and manipulation. Surveillance is not just watching; it's a form of power that restricts freedom and shapes behavior.

Surveillance Capitalism

Surveillance Capitalism

Tech giants like Google, Meta, and Amazon aren't just offering services - they're extracting behavioral surplus: data about your actions, preferences, and even intentions. This data isn't freely given - it's mined silently as you move, search, and scroll. The more they know about you, the more precisely they can predict and shape what you do next - not just to serve, but to sell, influence, and control.

Encrypt Everything

What it is: Encryption turns readable information (plaintext) into unreadable data (ciphertext). It's math that protects your messages.

Why it matters: Only the sender and intended recipient can read the content. No one else - not your ISP, not Big Tech, not governments.

How to do it: Use GPG (GNU Privacy Guard), a free and open-source encryption tool that lets you encrypt files, emails, and verify identity.

Encrypt Everything

GPG powers secure communication for developers, journalists, and activists. It's how you protect your ideas, your identity, and your freedom.

Learn more at gnupg.org

What is a GLUG?

A GNU/Linux Users' Group (GLUG) is a community dedicated to promoting and supporting Swatantra (Free/Libre) Software.

GLUGs bring together enthusiasts, developers, and advocates to share knowledge, collaborate on projects, and spread awareness about free and open-source software.

Without a central authority, the GNU/Linux movement relies on local groups like Mumbai GLUG to drive advocacy, education, and user support - empowering individuals to take control of their computing.
